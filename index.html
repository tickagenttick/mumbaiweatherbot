Style 05 — Variations of Blink, Persistence & Color

Eight riffs on the thin-outline indicator. Each one plays with a different frequency (how many pulses), persistence (how long the attention lasts), and color (neutral vs. accent). Click Simulate Language Change to see them all trigger at once, or pick a specific language.

Try:
A — Fast Double Blink
The baseline. Two crisp white flashes, then immediately calm. Quick and confident.
freq: 2 pulses duration: 1.2s color: white
MARATHI
B — Slow Breathing Pulse
A single, drawn-out swell. Feels serene — suits meditative or cinematic content.
freq: 1 pulse duration: 2.4s color: white
MARATHI
C — Triple Rapid Blink
Three staccato flashes — hardest to miss. Best when the language change is critical.
freq: 3 pulses duration: 1.5s color: white
MARATHI
D — Amber Alert
Warm amber flashes, like an Apple notification dot. Draws the eye without feeling urgent.
freq: 2 pulses duration: 1.4s color: amber #ff9f0a
MARATHI
E — Cyan Soft Glow
One bright bloom that lingers and slowly decays. Most cinematic of the set.
freq: 1 pulse duration: 2.8s color: cyan #64d2ff
MARATHI
F — Red Accent
Bold red double-flash with a faint fill wash. Highest stopping power — use sparingly.
freq: 2 pulses duration: 1.3s color: red #ff453a
MARATHI
G — Morse Pattern (· · —)
Two short, one long. A rhythmic signature the viewer subconsciously learns to recognize.
freq: 2 short + 1 long duration: 1.8s color: white
MARATHI
H — Persistent Afterglow
Sharp onset, then a long slow decay (>3s). Feels like a lighthouse sweep settling down.
freq: 1 pulse duration: 3.2s color: green #30d158
MARATHI
